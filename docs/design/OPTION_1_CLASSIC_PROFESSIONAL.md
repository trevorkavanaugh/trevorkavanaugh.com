# Design Option 1: Classic Professional

## Overview
Traditional banking aesthetic with conservative color palette and formal structure. Emphasizes trust, stability, and established expertise.

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Navy Blue `#1B3A57`
- **Secondary:** Charcoal Grey `#3C4043`
- **Accent:** Warm Tan `#C4A574`
- **Background:** White `#FFFFFF`
- **Text:** Dark Grey `#2D2D2D` (body), Navy `#1B3A57` (headings)

### Typography
- **Headings:** Merriweather or Georgia (serif for traditional feel)
  - H1: 48px, Navy
  - H2: 36px, Navy
  - H3: 24px, Charcoal
- **Body:** Open Sans or Arial (sans-serif for readability)
  - Body: 18px, Dark Grey
  - Line height: 1.6

### Visual Style
- Formal, corporate aesthetic
- Subtle textures (light background patterns)
- Professional stock photography (handshakes, boardrooms, documents)
- Minimal icons, when used: line-style, navy color
- Generous margins and padding

## User Experience

### Navigation Behavior
- Sticky header on scroll
- Smooth scroll to sections (single page) or page transitions
- Active page/section highlighted
- Mobile: Hamburger menu

### Interactive Elements
- Buttons: Solid navy (primary), tan outline (secondary)
- Hover states: Slight color darkening, shadow
- Form inputs: Clean, bordered, focus state
- Cards: Subtle elevation on hover

### Responsive Breakpoints
- Mobile: < 768px (single column)
- Tablet: 768px - 1024px (adjusted spacing)
- Desktop: > 1024px (full layout)

## Content Tone

### Writing Style
- Formal but approachable
- Industry-specific terminology (defined when necessary)
- Focus on expertise and results
- Third-person or first-person professional

### Key Messages
1. Specialized third-party risk expertise
2. Banking industry experience
3. Scalable, practical frameworks
4. Independent, objective guidance
5. Senior-level engagement

## Pros of This Design
- Highly credible and trustworthy
- Aligns with traditional banking aesthetic
- Professional without being cold
- Clear information hierarchy
- Familiar structure for target audience

## Cons of This Design
- May feel conservative/traditional
- Less distinctive or memorable
- Could blend in with larger firms
- Serif fonts may feel dated to some

## Best For
Consultants who want to emphasize:
- Established expertise
- Traditional banking relationships
- Conservative, risk-averse approach
- Corporate credibility
