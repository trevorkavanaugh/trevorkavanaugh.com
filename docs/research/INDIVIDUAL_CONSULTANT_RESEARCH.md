# Individual & Boutique Consultant Website Research

## Focus
Analysis of individual consultants and small boutique consulting firms (NOT Big 4 firms) to understand design patterns appropriate for a solo consulting practice.

## Sites Analyzed

### Boutique Risk Consulting Firms
1. **Ubiquitous Risk** - Boutique risk management consulting firm (UK, founded 2014)

### Individual Consultants (Various Specialties)
2. **Path for Growth** - Business coaching and team training
3. **Katie Little** - Copywriting and planning
4. **Blue Growth Advisors** - Financial services consulting
5. **Media Club** - Podcast production services
6. **VanRein Compliance** - Data security and compliance consulting

## Key Findings for Individual/Small Firm Websites

### Design Philosophy

**People Buy from People, Not Brands**
- Individual consultant websites can be more personal and less corporate
- Authenticity matters more than polish
- Professional headshots and personal story build connection
- Balance professionalism with approachability

**Minimum Viable Website Strategy**
- Launch quickly with well-defined strategy
- Start simple, iterate based on feedback
- Focus on clarity over complexity
- Don't over-engineer the first version

### Common Design Patterns

**1. Simplicity & Clarity**
- Intentionally simple, uncluttered layouts
- Effective use of white space
- Prioritize readability over visual complexity
- Clean, modern aesthetic without being trendy

**2. Color Schemes (Examples)**
- **Ubiquitous Risk:** Red/coral accent (#e73a46) + Navy blue (#1d3557) + White
- **VanRein Compliance:** Purple/aqua palette with textures
- **Blue Growth Advisors:** Ocean/marine theme (blues and teals)
- **Common Pattern:** 1-2 primary colors + white background + strategic accent

**3. Navigation Structure**
- Simple horizontal menu (5-6 items max)
- Typical structure:
  - Home
  - About / Who I Am
  - Services / What I Do
  - Insights / Blog / Resources
  - Contact
  - Optional: Case Studies, Testimonials, Careers

### Homepage Structure

**Hero Section Approaches:**

1. **Minimal Text, Big Impact**
   - Example: "Organisations Expand Prosper from intelligent risk taking rather than risk avoidance"
   - Clear value proposition in 1-2 sentences
   - Primary CTA button
   - Optional: Professional headshot or minimal imagery

2. **Service-Focused**
   - Brief introduction
   - Immediate display of core services
   - "Now booking" or availability indicator

**Content Sections (Typical Order):**
1. Hero with value proposition
2. Brief "About" or credentials summary
3. Services (3-5 core offerings)
4. Testimonials or case studies
5. Insights/thought leadership
6. Contact/CTA

### Service Presentation

**Card-Based Layout:**
- 3-5 service cards on homepage
- Each card includes:
  - Service title
  - 1-2 sentence description
  - "Learn More" link
- Grid layout (3 columns desktop, 1 column mobile)

**Detail Approach:**
- Homepage = overview
- Separate pages for detailed service descriptions
- Focus on client benefits, not just features
- Use "you-focused" writing addressing client needs

### Trust-Building Elements

**For Individual Consultants:**
1. **Professional Headshot** - Essential for personal connection
2. **Credentials & Experience** - Brief but specific
3. **Client Testimonials** - Real quotes with names (if possible)
4. **Case Studies** - Specific results and outcomes
5. **Thought Leadership** - Blog posts, articles, insights
6. **Transparency** - Some firms show pricing, process, timeline

**What Works:**
- "Years of experience" specifics
- Named clients (with permission) or industry sectors
- Certifications and specialized expertise
- Academic credentials (if relevant)

### Content Strategy

**Resource-Rich Approach:**
- Active blog with practical insights
- Case studies demonstrating expertise
- Free resources (guides, templates, assessments)
- Newsletter signup option

**"You-Focused" Writing:**
- Address client pain points directly
- Explain benefits, not just features
- Use clear, jargon-free language (or define technical terms)
- Focus on outcomes and results

**Website as Destination:**
- Dynamic, regularly updated content
- Relevant, engaging material
- Thought leadership positioning
- Pre-qualifier for ideal clients

### Design Sophistication Levels

**3 Approaches Observed:**

1. **Ultra-Minimal** (Katie Little style)
   - Minimal colors, simple layout
   - Conversational tone
   - Personal brand focus
   - Quick to build and maintain

2. **Professional-Modern** (Ubiquitous Risk style)
   - Clean, corporate aesthetic
   - Strategic color use
   - Balanced text and white space
   - Credible without being stuffy

3. **Creative-Thematic** (Blue Growth Advisors style)
   - Thematic visual design related to niche
   - More colorful and distinctive
   - Personality-driven
   - Memorable branding

### Technical Considerations

**Platforms Used:**
- Wix (common for ease of use)
- WordPress (for more control)
- Custom HTML/CSS (for uniqueness)
- Webflow (modern, design-focused)

**Must-Haves:**
- Mobile responsive design
- Fast load times
- Clear calls-to-action
- Contact form or scheduling link
- Professional domain name

**Nice-to-Haves:**
- Blog functionality
- Newsletter integration
- Client portal/login
- Online scheduling (Calendly, etc.)
- Analytics tracking

### Specific Recommendations for Bank Risk Management Consultant

Based on boutique/individual consultant research:

**Design Approach: Professional-Modern**
- Clean, corporate aesthetic (like Ubiquitous Risk)
- Navy blue primary + grey + tan/warm accent
- Minimal but sophisticated
- Trust-building without being impersonal

**Navigation Structure:**
```
Home | About | Services | Insights | Contact
```

**Homepage Sections:**
1. Hero: "Building Risk Management Frameworks That Scale"
2. Introduction: 2-3 sentences about specialization
3. Core Services: 3-5 service cards
4. Why Work With Me: Credentials, experience, approach
5. Client Success: Brief case study or testimonial
6. Latest Insights: 2-3 recent blog posts
7. Contact CTA: "Let's discuss your risk management needs"

**Color Palette Options:**

*Option 1: Navy & Tan (Traditional Banking)*
- Primary: Navy #1B3A57
- Secondary: Charcoal Grey #3C4043
- Accent: Warm Tan #C4A574
- Background: White #FFFFFF

*Option 2: Navy & Coral (Modern Professional)*
- Primary: Navy #1D3557
- Secondary: Slate Grey #4C607B
- Accent: Coral/Red #E73A46
- Background: White #FFFFFF

*Option 3: Multiple Blues (Conservative)*
- Primary: Navy #1B3A57
- Secondary: Corporate Blue #5D7B9A
- Accent: Light Grey #E8E9EB
- Background: White #FFFFFF

**Typography:**
- Headings: Modern sans-serif (Open Sans, Roboto, Inter)
- Body: Readable sans-serif, 16-18px
- Hierarchy: Clear H1 > H2 > H3 structure

**Content Priorities:**
1. Clear specialization in third-party risk
2. Emphasis on scalability ("frameworks that scale")
3. Banking industry expertise
4. Independence and objectivity
5. Practical, actionable insights

**Differentiation from Big 4:**
- More personal, accessible communication
- Faster response and flexibility
- Specialized focus (not generalist)
- Direct access to senior expertise
- Boutique service quality
