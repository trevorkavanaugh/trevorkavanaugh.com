Advisory Services

Helping financial institutions build third-party risk programs that survive regulatory scrutiny and real-world incidents.

I've built and run TPRM programs, not just assessed them. Managing portfolios of 500+ vendors with minimal staff means automation and systems thinking aren't nice-to-haves - they're survival. I've operated inside regulatory pressure, been through the exams, and know what holds up under scrutiny versus what looks good on paper.

My focus areas: software supply chain risk, concentration risk, and building programs that actually work. Not compliance theater. Not frameworks that exist to satisfy auditors while missing real risk. Programs that find their own issues before examiners do.

How I Can Help

01

TPRM Program Build-Out

For institutions building or rebuilding their vendor risk function. Policy development, procedure design, vendor tiering methodology, and automation strategy. Designed to scale - not create bureaucracy.

Deliverable: Complete program documentation and implementation roadmap
02

Regulatory Preparation

Pre-exam readiness assessment focused on third-party risk. Identify gaps examiners will find before they find them. Includes mock Q&A and documentation review.

Deliverable: Exam readiness report with remediation priorities
03

Ongoing Advisory Retainer

For risk leaders who want a practitioner on call. Monthly or quarterly access for ad-hoc guidance on vendor incidents, regulatory interpretation, program evolution, and the "is this normal?" questions that come up when you're running a real program.

Deliverable: Direct access via scheduled calls and async communication

Who This Is For

  • Community banks and regional institutions ($1B-$50B) building or maturing TPRM
  • Institutions facing regulatory criticism on third-party oversight
  • Risk leaders who want practical programs, not consultant frameworks
  • Organizations concerned about software supply chain exposure after recent industry incidents

30-minute call to discuss your situation. No pitch deck - just a conversation about what you're facing and whether I can help.
