The $10 Billion Wall: Why Your TPRM Program Doesn't Need to Look Like JPMorgan's

November 4, 2025 • 5 min read

There's a dangerous assumption in third-party risk management circles: if your TPRM program doesn't resemble what the largest banks are doing, you're falling behind. I've seen regional banks and credit unions spend hundreds of thousands of dollars trying to replicate frameworks designed for institutions managing 10,000+ vendor relationships when they have 200.

This isn't just wasteful—it's counterproductive. Let me explain why your institution's size is actually your competitive advantage when it comes to TPRM.

The Enterprise Trap

JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America, and Wells Fargo operate at a scale that requires industrial-strength vendor risk management. They need automated workflows, dedicated teams of 50+ people, and sophisticated GRC platforms that cost millions annually. Their regulatory scrutiny is intense, their vendor portfolios are massive, and their risk tolerance is nearly zero.

But here's what most consultants won't tell you: that complexity creates its own problems. Large banks struggle with vendor risk management precisely because of their scale. Their programs are slow to adapt, expensive to maintain, and often generate more bureaucracy than actual risk reduction.

What Actually Matters for Community Banks

If you're managing 200-500 vendor relationships, you don't need a $2 million GRC platform. You need clear processes, strong documentation, and the right risk-based categorization. The fundamentals that actually reduce risk:

  • Risk-based vendor tiering that focuses attention where it matters
  • Standardized assessment templates that your team can actually use
  • Clear escalation paths for issues that need senior attention
  • Ongoing monitoring that's proportional to vendor criticality
  • Documentation practices that satisfy regulators without drowning your team

Notice what's missing? Million-dollar software platforms. Dedicated teams of analysts. Automated AI-driven risk scoring that nobody understands.

The Right-Sized Approach

I worked with a regional bank last year that was spending $400,000 annually on a GRC platform they barely used. After implementing a streamlined framework using tools they already had (Excel, SharePoint, and a simple workflow system), they reduced assessment time by 40% and improved regulatory compliance. The total cost? About $75,000 in consulting fees and 20 hours of internal training.

"The goal isn't to look like JPMorgan. The goal is to build a program that actually works for your institution's size, complexity, and risk profile."

Building Frameworks That Scale

The real question isn't "What does JPMorgan do?" It's "What do regulators expect from an institution of my size, and what's the most efficient way to meet those expectations?"

Start with the OCC, FDIC, and Federal Reserve guidance on third-party risk management. These documents are surprisingly practical when you read them carefully. They don't mandate specific tools or team sizes. They require risk-based oversight, clear accountability, and ongoing monitoring. You can achieve all of that without enterprise-level complexity.
